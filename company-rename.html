Key Takeaways:

  1. Real words repurposed beat invented words (Amazon, Apple, Canopy)
  2. Short and memorable ‚Äî 1-3 syllables is the sweet spot
  3. Metaphorical resonance ‚Äî the name doesn’t describe what you do, it evokes what you aspire to
  4. Category-agnostic ‚Äî great names work across pivots (Amazon started selling books)
  5. Visual/brandable ‚Äî can you picture it on a building, on equipment, on a NYSE ticker?

3. Candidate Names: Full Evaluation

DOMAIN AVAILABILITY REALITY CHECK

After checking 300+ domains via WHOIS, the .com landscape is extremely saturated. Virtually every real English word .com is registered. The viable paths are:

  1. Buy a parked/for-sale domain ‚Äî many premium word .coms are on domain marketplaces (Sedo, Afternic, Efty, Fabulous, NameFind). Prices typically $2K-$50K for good words, sometimes more.
  2. Register an available coined word ‚Äî more limited but possible.
  3. Acquire from a small/inactive company ‚Äî some .coms are registered but not actively used.

  • Sounds like a real word ‚Äî elegant, Latin-rooted

10. TERAVAR

6. Methodology Notes

  • 300+ domains checked via WHOIS (verisign-grs.com registry)
  • 40+ web searches for trademark conflicts and existing company usage
  • Multiple domain marketplace indicators checked: Sedo, Afternic, Efty, Fabulous, ParkLogic, BrandBucket, NameFind, HugeDomains, BuyDomains, CashParking
  • Categories explored: Nature/earth, precision/surveying, agriculture terms, mythology, Latin/Greek roots, foreign languages, invented words, compound words
  • Key finding: Virtually every single-word English .com domain is registered. The question isn’t “which is available?” but “which parked domain is worth buying?”
