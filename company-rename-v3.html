1. Methodology

Round 3 was conducted with extreme rigor after Rounds 1–2 failed (Round 1: too literal/farming, Round 2: recommended Vexor which has an ACTIVE "Vexor Technology" trademark).

For EVERY candidate, the following was verified:

  1. Searched "[name]" site:trademarks.justia.com — zero trademark hits
  2. Searched "[name]" trademark registered technology — zero relevant hits
  3. Searched "[name]" company technology — zero active tech companies
  4. Searched "[name]" company OR inc OR llc OR ltd — zero relevant hits
  5. Checked .com domain status

2. The Hard Truth: Market Saturation

The single-word tech name space is extremely crowded

The single-word, 5–7 letter coined/abstract company name space is extremely saturated. Of ~85 names researched:

  • ~70% had active trademark registrations in tech/software/electronics
  • ~20% had active companies (even if no formal trademark)
  • ~10% were genuinely clean

Every "obvious" tech-sounding name is taken. Names ending in -ex, -ix, -on, -or, -er, -al, -en are the most saturated. Finding a clean name requires truly novel combinations.
